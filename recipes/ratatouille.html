Ratatouille

Description

Ratatouille (pronounced rat-uh-TOO-ee) is a very healthy and low fat summer vegetable stew. It is a great way to use up the abundant roundup of fresh summer vegetables from the garden. The perfection of ratatouille lies in the simplicity of the wholesome and fresh ingredients. It can be enjoyed as a main dish served over rice with some good crusty bread or you can enjoy as a side dish with a meat or fish entree. The most common ingredients for this stew are onions, garlic, zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and tomatoes. You could also add in additional vegetables from your garden to enjoy in this stew.

Ingredients

  • One (6 oz) can of tomato paste
  • 1/2 an onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Â¾ cup water
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 small eggplant, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, cored and very thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cored and very thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves, or to taste
  • 3 tablespoons mascarpone cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. Spread tomato paste into the bottom of a 10x10-inch baking dish. Sprinkle with onion and garlic
    and stir in 1 tablespoon olive oil and water until thoroughly combined. Season with salt and black pepper.
  3. Arrange alternating slices of eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, and yellow bell
    pepper, starting at the outer edge of the dish and working concentrically towards the center. Overlap the
    slices a little to display the colors. Drizzle the vegetables with 3 tablespoons olive oil and season with salt and black pepper.
    Sprinkle with thyme leaves. Cover vegetables with a piece of parchment paper cut to fit inside.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until vegetables are roasted and tender, about 45 minutes. Serve with dollops of mascarpone cheese.
